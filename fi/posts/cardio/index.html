What is Cardio?

huhtikuuta 10, 2022 2 lukuminuuttia
Fitness

Simply put, a cardiovascular exercise or cardio is an exercise that elevates your heart and breathing rates - effectively pumps your heart rate up to 60% - 70% of its maximum - for at least 20 minutes, improving your heart, lungs, and circulatory system.

Types of Cardio Link to heading

  • Steady State (SS)
    Exercising at a continuous, steady effort.

  • High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
    Switching between short periods of intense exercise and brief periods of recovery.

  • Intervals (effectively a mix of SS and HIIT)
    A repeated series of high intensity workouts mixed with rest or relief periods.

  • Fartlek
    Like interval training but far more random than structured, and very well-suited for outdoors cycling.

Some benefits of Cardio Link to heading

There are numerous benefits of cardiovascular exercise, but I would highlight the following:

  • Improved heart health
    The heart is a muscle just like any other in your body. If you fail to train it, it will weaken over time. By regularly getting your heart pumping at a faster rate you will keep it healthy and in shape.

  • Improved lung capacity
    Breathing throughout a cardio exercise can be tough. Pushing your breathing ability to the limit during an exercise will increase your lung capacity and improve the overall health of your lungs.

  • Mood lifting
    Our moods fluctuate daily but staying active helps boost your mood and reduce stress, especially after a stressful day. It improves your self-esteem and releases, a.o., the tension-fighting hormones dopamine and serotonin. During exercise you are challenging yourself mentally. There will be many times when you feel like you want to give up. However, pushing through and eventually finishing gives you a sense of accomplishment and you will feel better about yourself and improve your confidence.

  • Weight control and fat loss
    This is a very complicated subject. While you do burn calories while exercising, most people negate that quickly if they don’t make healthy dietary choices.

How much Cardio? Link to heading

This depends on the person as every person is unique. For me it depends on my goals and the time of year. Winters can be cold, and that brings a whole lot of other annoyances along with it besides the foul weather alone, like cold extremities. In general I work out 5 times per week. If I go outside, then 2 - 3 hours minimum, more if the weather permits it. If I stay indoors, then 1 - 1,5 hours on an exercise bike.
