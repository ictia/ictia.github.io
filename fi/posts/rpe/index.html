Rating of Perceived Exertion - RPE

Ideally you would measure exercise intensity by monitoring your heart rate. However, not everyone has a heart rate monitor readily available. You could measure it by stopping and taking your pulse at regular intervals…But, there is an alternative that works remarkably well: the Rating of Perceived Exertion, or RPE, also known as the Borg scale.

A Rating of Perceived Exertion is perhaps the most basic way to express the intensity you are experiencing during an exercise, and yet for all its simplicity, it has great value.

RPE Level of exertion Description Breathing
1 extremely easy watching TV and eating bonbons restful breathing, can sing
2 very easy getting up to make a cup of tea can talk in complete sentences
3 easy easy recreational riding can talk in broken sentences
4 moderate still comfortable, breaking a sweat talking first becomes difficult
5 somewhat hard just above comfortable, sweating more heavy breathing begins
6 moderately hard definitely sweating deep breaths, talking is avoided
7 hard sweating like a pig deep and forceful breathing
8 very hard grunt in response to questions labored breathing, can’t talk
9 very, very hard feels like you’re probably going to die very labored breathing
10 extremely hard yah dead? ya man! gasping for air
