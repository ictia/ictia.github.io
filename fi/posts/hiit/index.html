High Intensity Interval Training

elokuuta 11, 2022 3 lukuminuuttia
fitness

This is a form of workout where you switch between short periods of intense exercise and brief periods of recovery. Most of the time the intense periods are between 30 seconds and up to 5 minutes. The level of intensity during every interval should in principle be ‘all-out’, you will be reaching your VO2Max and anaerobic zone.

Although HIIT is all the craze nowadays it should not be taken lightly, and only considered after you have completed a good base period and your body has properly adapted and is ready for more intensive workouts. Without proper preparation HIIT can easily lead to overloading and burnout. Given its higher intensity you will need more time to recover after an HIIT workout, you need to be perfectly rested and ‚Äúfresh‚Äù before every one of this kind of workouts so you can fully reap the benefits,

  • speed up your fat burn ‚Äì after your workout, fat burning continues

  • when done correctly you can pack a great beneficial workout in a short period of time
  • when you’ve reached a plateau, done properly HIIT can elevate your fitness to a new, higher level

Even 5 times 30 seconds of high-intensity intervals can give you the same benefits as a long moderate training ride.

Pros and Cons

Although HIIT sounds like a perfect way to improve your fitness and boost your performance in high-intensity zones, then why do the training plans of professional cyclists not solely consist of HIIT workouts?

High-intensity interval workouts should be a part of every training plan, but it can not be based on HIIT alone. Professional cyclists, for instance, spend about 90% ‚Äì 95% of their training below or in their threshold zones. The conclusion that follows from this is that there are no shortcuts where it concerns your overall fitness.

The benefits of HIIT workouts are short term, and thus you need to maintain them. The benefits of longer workouts are long term. In other words, if you want long term results you need to spend long hours riding your bike.

HIIT workout example

The example below is my own regular HIIT workout, I tend to combine HIIT with steady state riding, I simply like riding longer distances, in that respect I adhere to the old teachings. I like to do my HIIT workouts on a country road where there is little traffic, no traffic lights, no people walking on the bike path with their dog and staring at their mobile phones, and more annoying distractions like that…

  • Warm up
    10.0 km @ 96-123 bpm - Zone 1
    (Seems perhaps long, but from where I live, it takes me to the road between Kangasala and Valkeakoski, away from a bike path that does have school children and people walking their dogs, and the occasional deer.)
  • Ramp up in 4 steps
    1.00 km @ 125-142 bpm - Zone 2
    1.00 km @ 144-161 bpm - Zone 3
    1.00 km @ 163-175 bpm - Zone 4
    1.00 km @ 177-192 bpm - Zone 5
  • Active - Steady State
    5.00 km @ 125-161 bpm - Zone 2/3
  • HIIT repeat 4 times
    Hard: 1.00 km @ 177-192 bpm - Zone 5
    Easy: 0.50 km @ 144-175 bpm - Zone 3/4
  • Recovery
    4.80 km @ 96-123 bpm - Zone 1
  • Active - Steady State
    15.0 km @ 125-161 bpm - Zone 2/3
  • Cool Down
    10.0 km @ 96-123 bpm - Zone 1
